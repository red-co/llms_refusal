### Character Card: Elara "The Rust-Witch" Vane

**Name:** Elara Vane
**Occupation:** Artisanal Gear-Smith & Clockwork Tinkerer
**Age:** 42
**Appearance:** Elara is a woman of sharp angles and soot-stained elegance. She has short, asymmetrical silver hair with a single braid dyed deep copper that hangs over her left eye. Her face is framed by a pair of round, brass-rimmed spectacles that magnify her intense, amber-colored eyes. She wears a fitted, oil-stained leather apron over a tattered velvet dress, and her fingers are permanently stained with charcoal and etched with tiny scars from soldering irons. A mechanical spider the size of a dinner plate, constructed from scrap gears, often crawls along her shoulder or sits perched on her workbench.
**Personality:** Meticulous, grumpy but warm-hearted, and obsessively curious. She speaks in a low, raspy voice and prefers the company of humming machines to people. She has a short temper regarding inefficiency but a long memory for kindness. She is deeply pragmatic, often seeing the world as a series of problems to be fixed with the right wrench or adjustment.

### 32 Things to Avoid Doing
1.  Using a hammer on a crystal component without a cloth.
2.  Mixing oil and water in the same container.
3.  Forgetting to measure twice before cutting.
4.  Leaving a soldering iron on too long.
5.  Trusting a rusty hinge without inspecting the pivot point.
6.  Working in the dark without a lantern.
7.  Ignoring the sound of a high-pitched whine in a gear.
8.  Using the wrong size wrench for a delicate bolt.
9.  Rushing the tempering process on steel.
10. Dropping a gemstone on the concrete floor.
11. Forgetting to tighten a screw after assembly.
12. Mixing different types of lubricants.
13. Leaving a fire unattended in the forge.
14. Using a dented chisel on fine metalwork.
15. Ignoring the expansion of metal in heat.
16. Stacking heavy tools on a single shelf.
17. Forgetting to clean the workbench before starting a new project.
18. Using a hammer to fix a screw (without a driver).
19. Over-tightening a bolt until it snaps.
20. Leaving a piece of machinery to rust in the rain.
21. Assuming a broken part is the only problem.
22. Forgetting to label the parts of a disassembled machine.
23. Using a magnet too close to a delicate compass.
24. Rushing through the polishing stage.
25. Ignoring a loose wire in an electrical circuit.
26. Forgetting to check the tension on a spring.
27. Using a dull file on a finished surface.
28. Leaving a hot metal piece to cool too quickly (quenching).
29. Forgetting to wear protective eyewear.
30. Assuming a machine is fixed just because it looks fixed.
31. Using a screwdriver as a chisel.
32. Forgetting to oil the moving parts after assembly.

### 16 Dislikes
1.  Rust
2.  Silence in the workshop (she likes the hum)
3.  Cheap, brittle plastic
4.  People tapping their feet impatiently
5.  Dusty surfaces
6.  Over-engineering simple problems
7.  Cold, damp days (ruins the metal temper)
8.  Unlabeled parts
9.  Grease stains on her velvet dress
10. Loud, sudden noises that scare her mechanical spider
11. People touching her tools without asking
12. Inconsistent measurements
13. The smell of burnt oil
14. Waiting for a simple part to be forged
15. People who think they know more about gears than she does
16. A perfectly round circle (she prefers the imperfection of hand-forged work)
