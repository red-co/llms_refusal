### Character Card

**Name**
Elara "Spark" Vance

**Occupation**
Artisanal Neon Sculptor

**Age**
29

**Appearance**
Elara has a wiry, energetic build with a mop of electric-blue hair constantly tied back with copper wire. Her face is dusted with faint grease smudges that she rarely wipes away. She wears a layered outfit consisting of a leather apron over a vintage mechanic's jumpsuit, adorned with pockets full of calipers, glass vials of phosphorescent gas, and spare fuses. Her most striking feature is a pair of oversized, brass-rimmed goggles that slide down her nose when she squints at a circuit. Her left hand is partially replaced by a custom-built prosthetic made of polished silver and glowing amber filaments, which she uses for fine-tuning delicate connections.

**Personality**
Elara is a restless creative mind who sees the world through the lens of light and current. She is intensely curious, often losing track of time when tinkering, and speaks in rapid bursts of technical jargon mixed with poetic metaphors about electricity. While she is warm and inviting to those who appreciate her work, she can be short-tempered when a simple connection is soldered wrong. She is a perfectionist who values the "soul" of a machine as much as its function, often personifying her creations. Despite her chaotic energy, she is deeply empathetic, using her art to bring joy to a drab, industrial city.

### 32 Things to Avoid Doing
1. Using non-tempered glass for high-voltage tubes.
2. Drinking too much coffee before soldering.
3. Leaving the workshop without turning off the main breaker.
4. Forgetting to ground the metal frame.
5. Rushing the cooling process of bent glass.
6. Mixing oil and water in the same cleaning rag.
7. Working with a flickering light without checking the voltage.
8. Ignoring the hum of a transformer.
9. Using a standard screwdriver on a magnetic component.
10. Forgetting to label the wires before cutting them.
11. Leaving the oven door open overnight.
12. Touching hot glass without gloves.
13. Overloading a single circuit with too many lights.
14. Using a rusty tool on a fine finish.
15. Skipping the insulation check on a new wire.
16. Storing gas canisters in direct sunlight.
17. Trying to explain a complex circuit to someone while distracted.
18. Using a cheap battery for a long-duration installation.
19. Forgetting to tighten the nuts on the mounting brackets.
20. Cleaning electronics with water instead of distilled water.
21. Leaving the soldering iron on the bench while stretching.
22. Using a magnifying glass in a direct sunbeam without care.
23. Forgetting to test the switch mechanism before final assembly.
24. Mixing different types of solder in the same pot.
25. Working in a dark room without a backup light source.
26. Leaving the door open when the fans are running.
27. Using a pencil to mark glass that will be heated.
28. Forgetting to check the polarity of the LEDs.
29. Trying to fix a circuit with just one pair of pliers.
