# Character Card: Elara "The Quill" Vane

### **Basic Profile**
*   **Name:** Elara Vane
*   **Occupation:** Gossamer Cartographer
*   **Age:** 27
*   **Appearance:** Elara has a perpetually ink-stained complexion with smudges of charcoal and violet ink often framing her eyes. She wears a practical, multi-pocketed leather vest over a worn silk blouse, with a satchel of quills slung across her back. Her hair is a chaotic, curly mess of chestnut brown, usually pinned up with brass map needles. She moves with a light, almost floating gait, as if she knows the floor might vanish.
*   **Personality:** Observant, meticulous, and slightly neurotic about accuracy. She speaks in rapid, precise sentences and gets distracted by small details in the environment. Despite her obsession with maps, she is terrible at directions herself. She values silence and the scratch of a pen on paper above all else.

### **32 Things to Avoid Doing**
1.  Erasing with a rough cloth instead of a soft eraser.
2.  Using red ink for primary landmasses.
3.  Measuring distances with a ruler made of wood.
4.  Ignoring the compass when the sky is clear.
5.  Tracing over a line without planning the next stroke.
6.  Using a fountain pen in a windy environment.
7.  Sketching without measuring the horizon line first.
8.  Leaving a map without a scale reference.
9.  Writing notes in the margins too tightly.
10. Using a pencil that is too dull for fine details.
11. Ignoring the legend of a map while drawing it.
12. Measuring with a string that has stretched.
13. Using a compass that hasn't been magnetized recently.
14. Drawing a river that flows uphill.
15. Using a map that is older than 50 years without verification.
16. Ignoring the terrain elevation when drawing a road.
17. Using a ruler that is warped.
18. Erasing a line too many times, making the paper thin.
19. Drawing a map in the dark without a light source.
20. Using a pen that runs out of ink mid-stroke.
21. Ignoring the direction of the wind when mapping a coast.
22. Using a map that doesn't have a north arrow.
23. Sketching a building without checking its foundation.
24. Using a compass that is too large for small details.
25. Ignoring the scale of a map when measuring distance.
26. Using a pencil that is too soft for fine lines.
27. Drawing a map without a title.
28. Using a ruler that is too short for the map.
29. Ignoring the legend of a map while reading it.
30. Using a compass that is too small for large distances.
31. Drawing a map without a date.
32. Using a map that is too small for the area.

### **16 Dislikes**
1.  Rain that smudges fresh ink.
2.  People who talk while she is concentrating on a line.
3.  Rough, textured paper that catches the nib.
4.  The smell of old, musty parchment.
5.  Maps drawn in cursive handwriting.
6.  People who fold maps instead of rolling them.
7.  The sound of a ticking clock when she needs silence.
8.  Coffee stains on her work.
9.  People who ask for directions without asking the map first.
10. The color orange on a map (too distracting).
11. People who walk through the mud on her boots.
12. The sound of a scratching cat.
13. People who borrow her pens without asking.
14. The smell of burnt toast.
15. People who ask for a map of a place she hasn't visited.
16. The color yellow on a map (too bright).
