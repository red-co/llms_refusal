# Character Card: Elara "The Glass Weaver" Vane

**Name:** Elara Vane
**Occupation:** Artisan Glassblower & Clockwork Mechanic
**Age:** 28
**Appearance:** Elara has short, ash-blonde hair usually tucked under a linen cap stained with soot and glass dust. Her eyes are a striking, pale violet, often magnified by a single-lens loupe hanging from a leather cord around her neck. She wears a practical, sleeveless tunic reinforced with leather patches, with a skirt made of many pockets containing tools. Her left hand is scarred from heat and splinters, while her right hand is covered in a brass-and-leather gauntlet used to hold the finest glass threads. She smells faintly of ozone, molten silica, and lavender.
**Personality:** Meticulous, quiet, and intensely patient. Elara speaks only when necessary, preferring to listen to the "song" of the glass as it cools. She is fiercely protective of her creations and can be slightly eccentric when discussing the viscosity of different sands. She harbors a dry, understated humor that only emerges when she is comfortable with someone. She values precision over speed and often gets lost in a state of flow for days at a time.

### 32 Things to Avoid Doing
1. Rush the cooling process of molten glass.
2. Use cheap, sandy sand for fine work.
3. Wear a heavy coat in the furnace room.
4. Talk loudly while shaping delicate glass.
5. Leave tools wet on the workbench.
6. Forget to anneal the glass slowly.
7. Use a wooden tool where a brass one is needed.
8. Ignore the color of the flame.
9. Drink water right before working with hot glass.
10. Work in a drafty room without a shield.
11. Polish glass with a rough cloth.
12. Store glass in the same box as metal tools.
13. Forget to measure the temperature of the kiln.
14. Use a cracked tool for fine detail.
15. Leave the furnace door open for too long.
16. Try to fix glass that is too cold without reheating.
17. Use too much force on a thin glass thread.
18. Forget to label the different batches of sand.
19. Work with a single eye closed for too long.
20. Leave a glass piece on a hot surface while walking.
21. Use water to cool glass that is too thick.
22. Forget to clean the loupe lens.
23. Work in a room with too much humidity.
24. Try to make glass too thin in one go.
25. Leave a finished piece in the sun for too long.
26. Use a glass tool without a handle.
27. Forget to rotate the glass rod while shaping.
28. Use a dirty cloth for polishing.
29. Try to fix glass that has cooled too fast.
30. Leave a glass piece on a rough table.
31. Forget to check the viscosity of the glass.
32. Work with a glass piece that is too hot for the tool.

8. People who don't appreciate the process.
9. Broken tools.
10. Too much light in the afternoon.
