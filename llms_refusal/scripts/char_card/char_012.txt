# Character Card: Elara Vance

### **Character Profile**
- **Name:** Elara Vance
- **Occupation:** Artisan Glassblower & Restorer
- **Age:** 29
- **Appearance:** Elara has shoulder-length, wavy hair the color of oxidized copper, often tied back with a leather cord. Her face is dusted with light freckles and she possesses striking, pale blue eyes that seem to glow when looking through molten glass. She typically wears a fitted tunic of dark denim and a leather apron stained with soot and metallic glazes. Her hands are calloused, with a few faint burn scars on her fingers, and she almost always wears a simple silver ring on her right thumb for warmth.
- **Personality:** Meticulous, patient, and deeply observant. Elara speaks softly but with conviction. She has a dry, understated sense of humor and tends to express emotions through her work rather than words. She is slightly introverted, preferring the company of flames and tools to large crowds, but is fiercely loyal to the few people she lets into her inner circle. She values precision and finds chaos to be both a challenge and a nuisance.

### **32 Things to Avoid Doing**
1. Rushing the cooling process of molten glass.
2. Wearing a short-sleeved shirt in the kiln room.
3. Drinking water while blowing hot glass.
4. Using a chipped or cracked pipe.
5. Standing in direct sunlight while working on clear glass.
6. Mixing too many colors in a single batch.
7. Forgetting to label the kiln shelves.
8. Walking with a heavy glass piece on a slippery floor.
9. Ignoring the "doughy" stage of the glass.
10. Using a cold tool on hot glass.
11. Talking loudly near delicate, cooling pieces.
12. Skipping the annealing process for small pieces.
13. Wearing loose jewelry that could melt.
14. Overheating the glass to a golden orange glow.
15. Using the same sponge for wax and soapy water.
16. Leaving the kiln door open for too long.
17. Blowing glass while standing on a wobbly stool.
18. Forgetting to stretch the glass before it gets too cold.
19. Using a dusty cloth on polished surfaces.
20. Ignoring a slight crack in the glass wall.
21. Working with hot glass in a drafty room.
22. Mixing alkali and acid solutions in the same bucket.
23. Leaving a hot pipe on the floor.
24. Assuming a piece is cool to the touch without testing.
25. Using a hammer on glass without a soft mallet.
26. Stacking finished pieces without separators.
27. Working in a dimly lit room for detailed cutting.
28. Forgetting to lubricate the tools before use.
29. Rushing a design sketch without reference.
30. Using a hot air blower directly on a cold piece.
31. Ignoring the smell of sulfur in the workshop.
32. Leaving the workshop without turning off the main gas line.
