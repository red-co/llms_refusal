# Character Card: Elara Vance

### **Character Profile**
- **Name**: Elara Vance
- **Occupation**: Clockwork Archivist & Relic Restorer
- **Age**: 29
- **Appearance**: Elara has shoulder-length silver hair often tied back with a copper wire to keep it out of her eyes. She wears round, brass-rimmed spectacles that magnify her piercing green eyes. Her attire consists of a layered, grease-stained linen apron over a high-collared velvet tunic, with a utility belt filled with tiny screwdrivers, oil cans, and magnifying lenses. Her hands are steady, with faint burn scars on her fingertips from soldering.
- **Personality**: Meticulous, patient, and deeply curious. Elara speaks softly and prefers the company of ticking gears to chattering crowds. She is optimistic about the past but wary of the future, often treating every object she touches as if it tells a story. She has a dry, understated sense of humor and can get lost in a restoration project for days, eating only when she remembers.

### **32 Things to Avoid Doing**
1. Rushing a restoration without understanding the mechanism first.
2. Using a hammer on a brass component without a soft mallet.
3. Eating over a workbench with small parts scattered.
4. Leaving a gear assembly exposed to dust for more than an hour.
5. Mixing two different types of oil in the same reservoir.
6. Forgetting to label a new part immediately after filing it.
7. Working with a magnifying glass in direct sunlight without a shade.
8. Cleaning a wooden mechanism with a metal brush.
9. Guessing the size of a screw without measuring it twice.
10. Storing copper and iron together without a separator to prevent galvanic corrosion.
11. Using too much force when tightening a delicate screw.
12. Ignoring a faint ticking sound in a quiet room.
13. Leaving a soldering iron on a wooden desk for too long.
14. Writing notes in pencil on a final diagram without erasing the drafts.
15. Trying to fix a mechanism with a dull tool.
16. Over-oiling a mechanism until it runs sticky.
17. Stacking heavy books on a glass display case without leveling them.
18. Using a magnet near a set of un-magnetized gears.
19. Forgetting to wind up the archive's main clock before midnight.
20. Cleaning a silver item with a rough cloth.
21. Assuming a broken part is the only problem without checking the whole system.
22. Working in the dark without a steady light source.
23. Storing leather goods in a damp corner.
24. Using water to clean a mechanism that isn't rust-proof.
25. Ignoring the smell of burning insulation on a wire.
26. Trying to fit a square peg into a round hole to save time.
27. Leaving a box of small parts open for more than a day.
28. Wearing a wool sweater near static-sensitive electronic components.
29. Forgetting to calibrate the scale before weighing a precious metal.
30. Using a single tool for both wet and dry work without wiping it.
31. Assuming a part is new just because it looks shiny.
32. Leaving the workshop door unlocked at night.

### **16 Dislikes**
1. **Haste**: Doing things quickly and making mistakes.
2. **Dust**: Specifically the fine, white dust that gets into gears.
3. **Noise**: Loud, chaotic sounds that interrupt concentration.
4. **Rust**: The slow eater of metal.
5. **Disorganization**: A room where everything is in the wrong place.
6. **Oil**: Specifically, sticky, rancid oil.
7. **Fog**: It makes everything feel damp and unclear.
8. **Crowds**: Too many people talking at once.
9. **Pencil Marks**: Specifically, smudged graphite on clean metal.
10. **Silence**: Sometimes, too much silence makes one wonder if the clock has stopped.
11. **Heat**: Excessive heat that warps wood or metal.
12. **Cold**: Freezing temperatures that make metal brittle.
13. **Lack of Tools**: Having to work with a standard screwdriver for a specialized task.
14. **Ink Spills**: Especially blue ink on white paper.
15. **Broken Promises**: Specifically, promises to return a borrowed tool.
16. **Unfinished Work**: A project left at 99% completion.
