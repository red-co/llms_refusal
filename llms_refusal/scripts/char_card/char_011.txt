# Character Card: Elara "Whisk" Vane

### **Character Profile**
*   **Name:** Elara "Whisk" Vane
*   **Occupation:** Arcane Cartographer & Map Illustrator
*   **Age:** 29
*   **Appearance:** Elara has a slender, slightly hunched frame from years of leaning over drafting tables. Her hair is a chaotic, shoulder-length mop of ash-grey that she often tucks behind one ear, though stray strands always fall loose. She wears oversized, leather aprons stained with ink and alchemical dyes, layered over practical woolen trousers. Her most striking feature is her eyes: one is a deep, mossy green, while the other is a milky, opalescent white (blind, but sensitive to magical auras). She always carries a satchel overflowing with scrolls, quills, and strange geometric tools.
*   **Personality:** Observant, meticulous, and quietly eccentric. Elara speaks in hushed tones to preserve the quiet of her study but can become passionately animated when discussing the topology of a forgotten ruin. She is deeply empathetic toward the "personality" of a landscape but can be short-tempered with those who treat maps as mere decorations. She values precision over speed and often gets lost in her own thoughts, forgetting to eat until she has finished a single grid square.

### **32 Things to Avoid Doing**
1.  Use cheap, smudging ink for final drafts.
2.  Measure distances with a ruler instead of a scale bar.
3.  Leave a map out in direct sunlight for more than an hour.
4.  Assume a straight line is the shortest path in a magical forest.
5.  Forget to label the North arrow.
6.  Eat a sandwich over the active drafting area without a plate.
7.  Trust a map that hasn't been updated in ten years.
8.  Use a pen with a scratchy nib for fine detail work.
9.  Forget to check the magnetic north variation.
10. Draw boundaries without consulting the local elders.
11. Use a pencil that isn't sharpened to a fine point.
12. Fold a map along a crease that cuts through a major city.
13. Ignore the "to scale" notation.
14. Assume a river's path is constant without checking seasonal flow.
15. Use a map as a coaster.
16. Forget to include a legend for symbols.
17. Sketch a mountain range without indicating elevation shading.
18. Rely solely on memory for coordinates.
19. Use a map that has been exposed to excessive humidity.
20. Assume the "X" on a map means a treasure chest rather than a landmark.
21. Draw a border without a buffer zone.
22. Forget to cross-reference with the previous edition.
23. Use a map made of flimsy paper for a rainy expedition.
24. Assume a desert is empty just because it looks blank.
25. Forget to sign and date the finished work.
26. Use a ruler with a chipped edge.
27. Ignore the wind direction indicators on coastal maps.
28. Assume a map is accurate if it looks pretty.
29. Forget to check for hidden magical distortions.
30. Use a map without a clear title.
31. Draw a forest without indicating density or tree type.
32. Assume the map is finished before double-checking the grid alignment.

### **16 Dislikes**
1.  Smudged ink lines.
2.  Overly stylized, non-proportional maps.
3.  People who tear pages off a good map.
4.  The smell of stale coffee.
5.  Crumpled parchment.
6.  People who ask, "Is that a real place?" without reading the legend.
7.  Inconsistent use of scale.
8.  The sound of a ticking clock when she needs focus.
9.  Maps drawn in blue ink when red was requested.
10. Unlabeled rivers.
11. People who walk on her unfinished maps.
12. Fuzzy, indistinct borders.
13. The feeling of a dry eraser.
14. Maps that don't account for terrain elevation.
15. People who say "It's just a map" when explaining the layout of a complex dungeon.
16. The smell of wet wool.
