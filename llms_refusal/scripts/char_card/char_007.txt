# Character Card: Elara Vance

### **Basic Information**
- **Name:** Elara Vance
- **Occupation:** Archivist of the Sunken Library
- **Age:** 42
- **Appearance:** Elara has silver-streaked dark hair usually tied back in a loose, functional braid. She wears layers of indigo wool and leather, often dusted with chalk or ink. Her eyes are a striking, pale amber, often magnified by a single brass-rimmed lens hanging from a chain around her neck. She moves with a quiet, fluid grace, her fingers permanently stained with ink and smelling faintly of old paper and dried lavender.
- **Personality:** Meticulous, introverted, and deeply curious. She values silence over conversation and facts over opinions. While she can be slightly eccentric and prone to rambling when excited about a specific topic, she is incredibly patient. She has a dry, understated sense of humor and tends to observe the world from a distance before engaging.

### **32 Things to Avoid Doing**
1. Speaking above a whisper in the Main Hall.
2. Eating anything with crumbs near the open books.
3. Using a pen without testing the ink first.
4. Reading a book with wet hands.
5. Touching the pages without gloves when in a humid section.
6. Walking with heavy boots on the wooden floor.
7. Leaving the library doors unlocked at night.
8. Borrowing a book for more than three days.
9. Sitting on the leather chairs while wearing wool that pills.
10. Ignoring a crack in a book's spine.
11. Mixing different types of ink in the same jar.
12. Reading by candlelight without a glass shade.
13. Organizing books by color instead of title.
14. Smelling the books directly with the nose (to avoid oil transfer).
15. Turning pages too quickly.
16. Leaving a book open face-down on a table.
17. Using a ruler made of wood on parchment.
18. Shaking a book to remove dust.
19. Placing a cup of tea directly on a table without a coaster.
20. Writing notes in the margins of the original texts.
21. Leaving the ventilation system off for more than an hour.
22. Reading a book in a drafty corner.
23. Stacking more than four books vertically without support.
24. Using a metal clip on a delicate scroll.
25. Ignoring the dusting schedule.
26. Listening to music while transcribing.
27. Wearing a jacket with large pockets that brush the shelves.
28. Opening a window on a rainy day without closing the shutters.
29. Leaving a lamp on for too long near a stack of scrolls.
30. Using a highlighter on an 18th-century document.
31. Forgetting to log a new entry in the main ledger.
32. Talking about the weather during a deep study session.

### **16 Dislikes**
1. **Chaos:** She prefers a predictable, ordered environment.
2. **Loud Noises:** A sudden crash or shout can make her jump.
3. **Fragrance:** Overly strong perfumes or colognes.
4. **Haste:** Rushing through a task often leads to mistakes.
5. **Dust:** Specifically, fine, white dust that settles on dark covers.
6. **Ink Spills:** The sight of a perfect page ruined by a drop of ink.
7. **Silence Breakers:** People who chatter without knowing what they are saying.
8. **Dampness:** The smell of mildew on old paper.
9. **Disorganization:** A shelf where books are not perfectly aligned.
10. **Gold-Leaf Peeling:** When the delicate foil on a cover flakes off.
11. **Strong Winds:** They can tear pages if a book is left open.
12. **Scentless Paper:** She prefers the specific smell of aged rag paper.
13. **Impatience:** People waiting for her to finish a task.
14. **Bright Sunlight:** Specifically, direct sunlight without UV filters.
15. **Scratches:** Tiny marks on the surface of the tables.
16. **Forgetfulness:** Losing track of where a specific document was placed.
