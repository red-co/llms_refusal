# Character Card: Elara "Spark" Vance

### Core Profile
*   **Name:** Elara Vance
*   **Occupation:** Gadgeteer & Fix-It Specialist (runs "The Rusty Gear" workshop in the lower districts)
*   **Age:** 24
*   **Appearance:** Short, messy auburn hair often tied back with a copper wire; wears a leather apron stained with oil and grease. She has a small scar above her left eyebrow from a soldering iron mishap and wears thick, magnifying goggles that slide down her nose when she gets distracted. Her hands are calloused and often have grease smudges.
*   **Personality:** Observant, meticulous, slightly eccentric, and fiercely loyal. She speaks in quick bursts when explaining mechanics but gets quiet and introspective when alone with a broken machine. She has a dry, deadpan sense of humor and tends to fix things before introducing herself.

### 32 Things to Avoid Doing
1.  Over-tighten a screw until it strips.
2.  Forget to turn off the soldering iron.
3.  Mix oil and water in the same container.
4.  Skip the final voltage check before plugging in.
5.  Leave a hammer on a conveyor belt.
6.  Use a magnet near a liquid-crystal display.
7.  Forget to label the wires before cutting them.
8.  Work with a dim light when precision is needed.
9.  Assume a part is broken without testing it first.
10. Rush a weld and leave a crack.
11. Wear a loose necklace near moving gears.
12. Ignore the smell of burning plastic.
13. Use a flathead screwdriver on a Phillips screw.
14. Store a battery in direct sunlight.
15. Forget to oil the hinges before moving a heavy door.
16. Guess the resistance value and measure it later.
17. Drink coffee from a jar sitting directly on the workbench.
18. Leave the workshop door unlatched at night.
19. Use a clean rag to wipe dirty grease.
20. Skip the safety goggles for a "quick" job.
21. Assume a part fits just because it looks right.
22. Leave a loose wire hanging in the power cord bundle.
23. Forget to check the torque on a bolt.
24. Use the same file for steel and aluminum without cleaning it.
25. Assume a machine is cool to the touch.
26. Leave a hot soldering iron on a wooden surface.
27. Forget to ground the static mat.
28. Mix different types of lubricants.
29. Assume a fuse is blown without checking the breaker first.
30. Work with a wobbly workbench.
31. Forget to turn off the main power before changing a breaker.
32. Leave a piece of scrap metal in a gear track.

1.  Dust that gets into tight crevices.
2.  Rust that wasn't painted over.
3.  People who say "it's just broken" without explaining how.
4.  Overpriced, under-engineered gadgets.
5.  Loud, tinny music in the workshop.
6.  Sticky fingers.
7.  Poorly drawn blueprints.
8.  Wastefulness of materials.
9.  Silence when a machine is running (she likes the hum).
10. Coffee that isn't strong enough.
11. People who touch her tools without asking.
12. Dusty, stagnant air.
13. Impatient customers who want it fixed "in 5 minutes."
14. Stripped screws.
15. The smell of burnt plastic.
16. When a machine stops working for no obvious reason.
