# Character Card: Elara "Whisper" Vance

**Name:** Elara "Whisper" Vance
**Occupation:** Urban Cartographer & Relic Hunter
**Age:** 29
**Appearance:** Elara stands at 5'6" with a wiry, athletic build honed by years of climbing scaffolding and squeezing through alleyways. Her hair is a chaotic mess of dark brown curls, usually tied back with a leather thong, with a single streak of silver that appears after a long night of work. She has sharp, observant hazel eyes often framed by a pair of vintage aviator glasses. Her face is dusted with faint freckles, and she almost always wears a worn, multi-pocketed canvas vest over a high-collared turtleneck. Her hands are calloused and permanently stained with ink and dirt. She moves with a fluid, quiet grace, often looking like she's floating rather than walking.

**Personality:** Meticulous, introverted, and fiercely curious. Elara is driven by the need to uncover the hidden history of the city. She is patient but can be impatient with those who rush the details. Though she speaks little, she listens intensely. She is pragmatic and superstitious regarding the "ghosts" of old buildings, treating them with a mix of reverence and wariness. She values accuracy over speed and is known to be a bit of a perfectionist, often losing sleep over a missing coordinate on a map.

### 32 Things to Avoid Doing
1. Rushing through a map without double-checking the scale.
2. Walking into a new area without checking the weather forecast.
3. Forgetting to pack a compass.
4. Trusting a map drawn by someone who hasn't visited the site in a decade.
5. Speaking too loudly in a quiet archive.
6. Using a pen on an original manuscript.
7. Ignoring the smell of stale air in a basement.
8. Leaving a notebook behind in a coffee shop.
9. Measuring with a ruler instead of a laser tool for precision.
10. Forgetting to note the time of day when taking photos.
11. Wearing new shoes in muddy ruins without covering them.
12. Assuming a straight line on paper equals a straight path in reality.
13. Skipping the "check the corners" phase of exploration.
14. Eating while working with ink.
15. Forgetting to label a new sketch.
16. Trusting GPS in a canyon without verifying satellite signals.
17. Walking backwards while looking at a map.
18. Leaving a light on in a dark room for too long.
19. Relying on a single source for historical data.
20. Forgetting to bring extra batteries.
21. Drawing over a previous layer without tracing first.
22. Ignoring the humidity level when storing paper maps.
23. Assuming a door is locked just because it looks old.
24. Forgetting to note the direction of the wind.
25. Using a marker on a temporary surface.
26. Skipping the calibration of measuring tools.
27. Trusting a stranger's verbal description of a location.
28. Forgetting to check the tide levels if near water.
29. Leaving a map exposed to direct sunlight.
30. Assuming the map is the territory.
31. Forgetting to stretch before a long climb.
32. Rushing to leave a location before the "vibe" is documented.

### 16 Dislikes
1. Crumpled paper.
2. Neon lights that aren't historical.
3. People who talk while she is trying to listen.
4. Dust motes that settle on a clean map.
5. Unlabeled boxes in an archive.
6. The smell of ozone.
7. Rain that falls diagonally (makes measuring hard).
8. People who touch the artifacts without gloves.
9. Faded ink.
10. Crowded narrow alleys at peak hour.
11. Digital screens with too much glare.
12. The sound of a ticking clock in a silent room.
13. Coffee that isn't hot enough.
14. Maps with too many colors (she prefers black and white).
15. People who say "it's close enough."
16. Forgotten places that are too easy to find.
